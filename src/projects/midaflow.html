Midaflow is an enterprise resource planning software system that helps small and medium-sized businesses manage their payments, invoices and vendors. In July 2023, I joined Midaflow during their early stages of product development. In the short time that I have worked for Midaflow, I played a pivotal role in designing key components of the minimum viable product that secured us our first client.

What is enterprise resource planning?

Enterprise resource planning (ERP) is a comprehensive software system designed to integrate and streamline various aspects of an organization's core business activities. It serves as a centralized hub that connects different departments and functions within the organization such as:

Different components of an ERP software system.

ERP software systems have traditionally been accessible only to large organizations with the resources to afford such comprehensive enterprise solutions. Recognizing a gap in the market, the founders of Midaflow believed that ERP systems should also be accessible and easy-to-use for small and medium-sized businesses. With this vision, they founded Midaflow, focusing their efforts on the financial components of ERP systems: Finance & Accounting, Analytics & Reporting, and Purchasing.

But what did I know about enterprise resource planning?

Prior to Midaflow, I had no idea what an ERP software system was. This would have posed a significant challenge for me when designing for this system. However, I did notice some similarities between ERP software systems and Shopify, for which I do have extensive knowledge and experience in.

Comparison between ERP software systems and Shopify.

Both systems integrate and streamline various aspects of core business activities, and serve as a centralized hub for their organization. This comparison helped me understand the core concept of an ERP software system.

Learning what works and what doesn't

Given the project's time constraints and the extensive research already conducted by the founders, further research was not a priority. So in order for us interns to get a better idea on what could be improved, we conducted discovery research by analyzing user feedback on our competitors. This allowed us to identify common strengths and weaknesses.

User feedback from a competing application.

Based on the feedback we reviewed, users value ERP systems for their comprehensive set of features and customization options that streamline operations, though challenges like high costs, complexity in customization, and varying customer support levels persist. They appreciate intuitive designs, reliable support, and streamlined integration processes to improve usability. However, concerns about feature overload and integration issues underscore the need for ERP systems to prioritize user experience enhancements, ensuring they meet operational needs effectively while maintaining user satisfaction.

The lifecycle of an invoice

A key responsibility I had for this project was designing components for the lifecycle of an invoice. However, I did not have any experience with the complete lifecycle of an invoice from both the end-user and issuer perspectives. My only experience with invoices was during my time running an eCommerce business where I regularly processed and paid invoices for goods purchased. Nothing too complex. Similar to my experience with ERP software systems, this would have posed a significant challenge when designing for this lifecycle. To address this issue, I mapped out and analyzed the entire invoice lifecycle, detailing the requirements and features for each stage.

[Lifecycle Image]

After mapping out and analyzing the invoice lifecycle, I have gained a comprehensive understanding for each stage of the lifecycle. This process has enabled me to identify the specific requirements and features needed at every point in the lifecycle for both the end-user and the issuer perspectives.
